Cristóbal Riego Hunt

writer · translator
cristobal.riego@gmail.com

Cristóbal Riego Hunt is a Chilean writer and translator based in Santiago. He holds an MFA in Nonfiction Writing from Columbia University, where he studied as a Fulbright Scholar, and was an editorial intern at The Paris Review. His translation of Daniel Saldaña París's short story “The Cascades” is forthcoming in The Paris Review issue 256 (Summer 2026), as is his translation of an essay by Mexican filmmaker Everardo González about True Crime documentary, in Documentary magazine. He is at work on a second novel.

  1. The Patron

    On a failed novelist in Valparaíso, the advertising man who financed his dream, and what it takes to tell him the truth.

  2. In Search of the Rabu-Ka

    On Benjamín Labatut, Lee Sedol, and whether any writer is ever truly the author of her own work.

  3. Lyrical Silicone

    On Pedro Lemebel, the Latin American crónica, and the cost of writing in a form the literary establishment cannot contain.

  4. Juan Emar's Literary Laboratory

    On Juan Emar's absurdist stories and the quiet war an eccentric writer wages against a country's official literature.
